About

How I got started...

  • 2002-2004

    My Humble Beginnings

    I started making websites in highschool for fun. I got so good at it that I was getting paying gigs for local businesses throughout my junior and senior year.

  • March 2011

    My Passion Reignites

    Still obsessed with computer science I ended up dropping out of college in my junior year to pursue a freelance career in web development.

  • October 2013

    Started Teaching at a Largest Local Tech Hub

    After freelancing for a number of years self taught, I felt like I had a unique perspective to share in the community I grew up in. I started teaching beginning and intermediate web development and have continued to on and off since 2013

  • November 2015

    Worked at HZDG

    I had the opportunity to work at HZDG, a top east coast based marketing/advertising company. I had the pleasure of working with big clients like Discovery Channel, Hilton Hotels, VW, Rockefeller Center, Philidelphia Eagles, Consumer Electronic Show and many more.

Discovery Channel

The Science of Soil

Discovery Channel tasked me and a graphic designer to create an interactive website to teach children the importance of Nitrogen, Phosphorus and Potassium in farming. It was the first "interactive" created for Discovery that was completely responsive and works on the tiniest phone to the largest desktop.

  • Tech Stack: HTML, SASS, Backbone.js, Marionette.js, Javascript, Nunjucks, Gulp, Webpack.
  • Client: Discovery Science

Consumer Electronic Show

Starting in 2017 and also 2018

Created an interactive map for CES in 2017 and they liked it so much I did it again in 2018. Using Photoshop & Illustrator I was able to import an image, draw over it, and export it as HTML SVG code. I was able to target specific areas/shapes with javascript to create functionality and hovers.

  • Tech Stack: HTML, SASS, SVG, SnapSvg.js, Photoshop, Illustrator, Javascript, Gulp, Webpack.

Urban Green Community

East Coast Apartment Complex

Based on an initial design I was able to create this completely responsive site in a matter of 2 weeks. The project came in under budget and when delivered was admired by the complex that hired us. I used Nunjucks as a templating language since the site could eventually get turned into having a python/django backend. And since I've worked in those environments before I knew that Django used the same templating language that I've used on other javascript projects.

  • Tech Stack: HTML, SASS, jQuery, Nunjucks, Gulp, Webpack.
  • Client: Urban Green Apartments
